# Bouillabaisse

## Summary
Bouillabaisse is a traditional Proven√ßal fish stew from Marseille, featuring a variety of fresh seafood in a saffron-infused broth. This luxurious dish is typically served with rouille (garlic mayonnaise) and crusty bread.

## Ingredients
**For the broth:**
- 2 lbs mixed fish (snapper, sea bass, monkfish), cut into chunks
- 1 lb mussels, cleaned
- 1/2 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 large onions, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 large tomatoes, chopped
- 1 fennel bulb, sliced
- 2 leeks (white part only), sliced
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1 cup white wine
- 6 cups fish stock
- Large pinch of saffron threads
- 1 bay leaf
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 strip orange zest
- Salt and pepper to taste

**For the rouille:**
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 cup olive oil
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- Salt to taste

## Preparation
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Add onions, leeks, and fennel. Cook until softened, about 10 minutes.
2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes until they break down.
4. Pour in white wine and let simmer for 3 minutes.
5. Add fish stock, saffron, bay leaf, thyme, and orange zest. Bring to a boil.
6. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes to develop flavors.
7. Season broth with salt and pepper. Remove bay leaf, thyme, and orange zest.
8. Add firm fish chunks first, cook for 5 minutes.
9. Add shrimp and mussels, cook for 3-4 minutes until mussels open and shrimp are pink.
10. For the rouille: In a food processor, blend garlic, egg yolk, and cayenne. Slowly drizzle in olive oil until thick and creamy. Season with salt.
11. Serve bouillabaisse in bowls with toasted bread and rouille on the side.
