# Coq au Vin

## Summary
Coq au Vin is a classic French dish where chicken is slowly braised in red wine with bacon, mushrooms, and pearl onions. This rustic and flavorful recipe originated in the Burgundy region and transforms simple ingredients into an elegant meal.

## Ingredients
- 1 whole chicken (3-4 lbs), cut into pieces
- 6 oz bacon, diced
- 2 cups red wine (Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
- 1 cup chicken stock
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 8 oz mushrooms, quartered
- 12 pearl onions, peeled
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 bay leaves
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 3 tablespoons butter
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley for garnish

## Preparation
1. Season chicken pieces with salt and pepper. In a large Dutch oven, cook bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove and set aside.
2. Brown chicken pieces in bacon fat on all sides (about 5 minutes per side). Remove and set aside.
3. Add pearl onions and carrots to the pot, cook for 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
4. Sprinkle flour over vegetables and stir. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.
5. Pour in wine and stock, scraping up browned bits. Add bay leaves and thyme.
6. Return chicken and bacon to pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes.
7. In a separate pan, saut√© mushrooms in butter until golden. Add to the pot.
8. Continue cooking uncovered for 15 minutes until chicken is tender and sauce has thickened.
9. Remove bay leaves and thyme sprigs. Adjust seasoning. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.
