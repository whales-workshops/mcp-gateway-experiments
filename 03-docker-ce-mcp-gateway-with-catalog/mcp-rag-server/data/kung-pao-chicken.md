# Kung Pao Chicken

## Summary
Kung Pao Chicken is a classic Sichuan dish featuring tender chicken pieces stir-fried with peanuts, vegetables, and dried chilies in a sweet, sour, and spicy sauce. This bold and flavorful dish delivers the perfect balance of heat and savory notes.

## Ingredients
- 1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 cup roasted peanuts
- 8-10 dried red chilies
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 4 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
- 2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil

**For the marinade:**
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch

**For the sauce:**
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons black vinegar (or rice vinegar)
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 2 tablespoons water

## Preparation
1. Mix marinade ingredients and toss with chicken cubes. Let marinate for 20 minutes.
2. Combine all sauce ingredients in a bowl and whisk until smooth. Set aside.
3. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a wok over high heat. Stir-fry chicken until cooked through, about 5-6 minutes. Remove and set aside.
4. Add remaining oil to wok. Add Sichuan peppercorns and dried chilies. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant (be careful not to burn).
5. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds.
6. Add bell peppers and stir-fry for 2 minutes until slightly softened.
7. Return chicken to wok. Add green onions.
8. Give sauce a quick stir and pour into wok. Toss everything together for 1-2 minutes until sauce thickens and coats everything.
9. Add roasted peanuts and toss to combine.
10. Serve immediately over steamed rice.
