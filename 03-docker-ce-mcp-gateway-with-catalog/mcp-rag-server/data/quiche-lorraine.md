# Quiche Lorraine

## Summary
Quiche Lorraine is a classic French tart from the Lorraine region, featuring a buttery crust filled with a rich custard, bacon, and cheese. This versatile dish is perfect for brunch, lunch, or a light dinner.

## Ingredients
**For the crust:**
- 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
- 3-4 tablespoons ice water

**For the filling:**
- 8 oz bacon, diced
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 1 1/2 cups Gruyère cheese, grated
- 4 large eggs
- 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
- 1/2 cup whole milk
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh chives for garnish

## Preparation
1. For the crust: Mix flour and salt. Cut in cold butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
2. Add ice water one tablespoon at a time until dough comes together. Form into a disk, wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
3. Roll out dough and fit into a 9-inch tart pan. Trim edges and prick bottom with a fork.
4. Line with parchment paper, fill with pie weights or dried beans. Blind bake at 375°F for 15 minutes.
5. Remove weights and parchment. Bake 5 more minutes until lightly golden. Let cool.
6. For the filling: Cook bacon in a skillet until crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
7. Sauté onion in 1 tablespoon of bacon fat until softened, about 5 minutes.
8. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, cream, milk, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
9. Sprinkle bacon, onion, and cheese evenly over the crust.
10. Pour egg mixture over the filling.
11. Bake at 375°F for 35-40 minutes until custard is set and top is golden brown.
12. Let cool for 10 minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh chives and serve.
