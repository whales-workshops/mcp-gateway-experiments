# Beef Bourguignon

## Summary
Beef Bourguignon is a sophisticated French stew featuring tender beef braised in red wine with vegetables and aromatics. This iconic dish from Burgundy is perfect for special occasions and develops deep, complex flavors through slow cooking.

## Ingredients
- 3 lbs beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
- 1 bottle (750ml) red Burgundy wine
- 4 oz bacon, cut into lardons
- 18 pearl onions, peeled
- 1 lb carrots, cut into chunks
- 8 oz button mushrooms
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 3 tablespoons flour
- 2 cups beef stock
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 2 bay leaves
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped

## Preparation
1. Season beef cubes generously with salt and pepper. Let stand at room temperature for 30 minutes.
2. In a large Dutch oven, cook bacon until crispy. Remove and reserve.
3. Brown beef in batches in the bacon fat over high heat, about 3-4 minutes per side. Set aside.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add carrots and pearl onions, cook for 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
5. Sprinkle flour over vegetables, stir and cook for 2 minutes. Add tomato paste and mix well.
6. Pour in wine and beef stock, scraping bottom of pot. Add bay leaves, thyme, and bacon.
7. Return beef to pot. Bring to a boil, cover, and transfer to a preheated 325°F oven.
8. Braise for 2.5 to 3 hours until beef is fork-tender.
9. Meanwhile, sauté mushrooms in butter until golden brown.
10. Remove pot from oven. Discard bay leaves and thyme. Stir in mushrooms.
11. If sauce is too thin, simmer uncovered on stovetop for 10-15 minutes to reduce.
12. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with crusty bread or mashed potatoes.
