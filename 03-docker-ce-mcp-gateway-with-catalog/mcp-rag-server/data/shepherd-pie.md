# Shepherd's Pie

## Summary
Shepherd's Pie is a hearty British classic featuring savory minced lamb in rich gravy topped with creamy mashed potatoes. This comforting dish is perfect for cold evenings and uses simple ingredients to create maximum flavor. Note: when made with beef, it's called Cottage Pie.

## Ingredients
**For the filling:**
- 2 lbs ground lamb (or beef for Cottage Pie)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 2 carrots, diced
- 2 celery stalks, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1 cup beef stock
- 1 cup red wine
- 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 1 cup frozen peas
- 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and pepper to taste

**For the topping:**
- 3 lbs potatoes, peeled and cubed
- 4 tablespoons butter
- 1/2 cup whole milk, warmed
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 egg yolk (optional, for browning)

## Preparation
1. Boil potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain well.
2. Mash potatoes with butter and warm milk until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground lamb and cook until browned, breaking up with a spoon, about 8 minutes. Drain excess fat.
4. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for 8 minutes until softened.
5. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.
6. Sprinkle flour over meat mixture and stir for 1 minute.
7. Pour in wine and stock, scraping up any browned bits. Add Worcestershire sauce, thyme, and bay leaf.
8. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes until sauce thickens. Stir in peas. Season with salt and pepper. Remove bay leaf.
9. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
10. Transfer meat mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish, spreading evenly.
11. Spread mashed potatoes over meat layer, creating peaks and swirls with a fork. Brush with egg yolk if using.
12. Sprinkle cheddar cheese over the top.
13. Bake for 25-30 minutes until top is golden brown and filling is bubbling.
14. Let rest for 10 minutes before serving.
